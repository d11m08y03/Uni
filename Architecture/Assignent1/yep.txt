You are required to (1) lay out your respective requirements, (2)
investigate all components of the PC, (3) select your motherboard,
and (4) label the main components on the motherboard. All the
selected components/parts should be “compatible” with the chosen
motherboard.
You also need to select an OS for your PC based on your
requirements that justifies any cost associated with the OS. Compare
and contrast with open source alternatives.

- Coding
- Hardware with Linux Support 
- Quiet
